New Tax Regime (Section 115BAC) · FY {{ currentYear }}-{{ currentYear + 1 }}

new_releases New Regime

The New Tax Regime (Section 115BAC) is the default tax system in India from FY 2023-24 onwards. It offers lower tax rates with fewer slabs, but most deductions like 80C, 80D, HRA, and home loan interest (self-occupied) are not allowed.

Key Features:
  • check_circle Standard deduction of ₹75,000 for salaried & pension income
  • check_circle Section 87A rebate: Zero tax if taxable income ≤ ₹12,00,000
  • check_circle Effectively zero tax up to ₹12.75L for salaried individuals
  • check_circle 4% Health & Education Cess on calculated tax
  • check_circle Surcharge applicable for income above ₹50 Lakhs
Deductions NOT Allowed:
• Section 80C (PPF, ELSS) • Section 80D (Health) • HRA Exemption • Home Loan Interest (Self) • Section 80E (Education) • LTA Exemption
Limited Deductions Allowed:
• Standard Deduction (₹75,000) • Employer NPS contribution (Sec 80CCD(2)) • Agniveer Corpus Fund (Sec 80CCH)

Section 87A Rebate

If your taxable income is ≤ ₹12,00,000, your tax is rebated to ₹0. For salaried individuals with the ₹75,000 standard deduction, this means zero tax up to ₹12,75,000 gross income.

Surcharge (High Income)

₹50L – ₹1Cr → 10% surcharge

₹1Cr – ₹2Cr → 15% surcharge

Above ₹2Cr → 25% surcharge (max for new regime)

Marginal relief may apply at threshold boundaries.
